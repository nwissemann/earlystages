early stages is my newsletter about second-order careers.

Most people choose careers based on first-order effects: salary, prestige, titles, speed.

And while that’s all fair, I want to encourage you to think in second order. Not just what happens next, but what happens after that.

Many early-career decisions are made by outsourcing thinking to what feels safe, impressive, or “default”. We are so busy playing society’s game that we forget to think whether it’s one we actually want to play.

early stages challenges that default mode. It’s a space for people who want to step off conventional paths, or at least play them intentionally.

It’s designed to help ambitious young careerists close two gaps:

  • inside–outside: what they value vs. what they do
  • present–future: where they are vs. where they want to go

At its core, early stages explores one question:

How do you make life & career decisions today that still make sense years from now?

That’s what second-order thinking is for.

what to expect?
